Adelie Penguins

These are adelie penguins. The Adélie penguin (Pygoscelis adeliae) is, along with the emperor penguin, the most common penguin along the Antarctic coast. They are among the seabirds that live the furthest south. They are named after Adélie Land, which refers to Adèle Dumont d'Urville, the wife of the French explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville, who in 1840 discovered these penguins.

Description

This penguin is a mid-sized penguin, where a grown-up adelie penguin will become about 46cm to 71cm tall, and 3.6kg to 6kg heavy. The tail is a little longer than other penguins' tails. The appearance looks somewhat like a tuxedo. They are a little smaller than other penguin species.
