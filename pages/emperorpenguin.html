Emperor Penguins

The emperor penguin (Aptenodytes forsteri) is the tallest and heaviest of all living penguin species lives in Antarctica. The male and female are similar in size, reaching 122 cm (48 in) in height and weighing from 22 to 45 kg (49 to 99 lb). The head is black in a strong contrast from it's white belly. It also has yellow lines along it's head.

It's diet consists of fish.

Emperor penguins is the only penguin species that breed during winter. The female lays a single egg, which is incubated by the male while the female returns to the sea to feed; parents subsequently take turns foraging at sea and caring for their chick in the colony. The typical lifespan of the emperor penguin is around 20 years in the wild, although observations suggest that some individuals may live to 50 years of age.
